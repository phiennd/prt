FRosalind

Antimicrobial resistance is one of the scariest slow-moving crises in global health. FRosalind treats resistance as a system rather than a single molecular event: enzymatic destruction, target modification, metabolic bypass, and reduced drug accumulation through efflux.

It is an AI-powered intelligence platform built on protein language models, differential geometry, and fitness landscape research. The hospital-facing product ranks antibiotic options with mechanistic reasoning, helping doctors make faster and more defensible decisions.

Bacterial Food Fermentation

In collaboration with Prof. Imroze Khan at the Trivedi School of Biosciences at Ashoka University, this project studies how bacterial ecology shapes fermentation outcomes. The questions sit at the intersection of evolutionary biology and food systems.

RAD-Scan

A radiation and heavy metal bioaccumulation monitoring project, built around lateral flow assays for γ-H2AX detection and a blockchain-based exposure reporting system. The idea is to give communities near nuclear waste sites the tools to measure their own risks, starting with Jadugoda in India. This was part of the Non-Trivial Fellowship in 2025.

Currently on pause while a deeper literature review is underway. The science is interesting enough to come back to properly.
